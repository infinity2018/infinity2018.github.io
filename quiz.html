We, the students of department of mathematics and statistics, have been organizing our fest named ‘INFINITY’ since 4 years. Our aim is to grow interest in mathematics, statistics and logical thinking. In this year, 2018, we add a new event ‘Master Mind’, logical event where everyone studying in undergraduate can participate in the event. Here is an event ‘Colloquium’ where selected participants can represent themselves on stage and face some questions by the audience and professors. The inauguration of the fest will commence with a short lecture of our Principal after the prelims at about 10:30 am. The fest will continue till 5pm.

In an educational context, a quiz is usually a form of a student assessment. We have organized a ‘Quiz’ event to check comprehension of a type of mathematical exercise. It will be conducted through two parts prelims (expected time of starting at 9:30 am) and ‘On stage rounds’ (expected time of starting at around 11:30 am). Each team should have two participants. On the basis of prelims six teams will be selected for finals.

The ‘Quiz’ event consists of five rounds. There will be no elimination for first round while in the next round there will be double elimination. At the end of third and fourth round there will be single elimination. At last, the final round will see the emergence of the winner.

Sample Questions

  1. If \(m\) is a positive integer such that \[\sum_{n=1}^{\infty }log\Big({1-\frac{1}{{(n+5)}^{2}}}\Big)=log{\left ( \frac{m}{m+1}\right )}\]what is the value of \(m\)?

    2. Answer

    The answer is \(m = 5\)

    Using \(log(a) + log(b) = log(ab)\) we have \[\prod_{n=1}^{\infty}\left ( 1- \frac{1}{(n+5)^2} \right ) = \frac{m}{m + 1}\]

    \[\Rightarrow \prod_{n=1}^{\infty}\left ( \frac{(n+4) (n+6)}{(n+5)^2} \right ) = \frac{m}{m + 1}\]

    \[\Rightarrow \prod_{n=1}^{\infty}\left ( \frac{n + 4}{n + 5} \right ) \prod_{n=1}^{\infty}\left ( \frac{n + 6}{n + 5} \right ) = \frac{m}{m + 1} .....(i)\]

    \[However \prod_{n+1}^{\infty}\frac{n + 4}{n + 5} = 5, and \prod_{n+1}^{\infty}\frac{n + 6}{n + 5} = \frac{1}{6} ........(ii) \]

    from \(i\) and \(ii\) we get \(\frac{m}{m + 1} = \frac{5}{6}\) and hence \(m = 5\)



  2. There are \(10\) boxes numbered \(1,2,3,…,10\) each containing \(2017\) white and \(2018\) black balls. A ball is drawn at random from \(Box-1\) and is put into \(Box-2\).Then a ball is drawn at random from \(Box-2\) and is put into \(Box-3\).This process is repeated until a ball is drawn from \(Box-9\) at random and is put into \(Box-10\).Then a ball is drawn at random from \(Box-10\) at random. What is the probability that this ball is black.

    3. Answer

    At first, we calculate the probability of drawing a black ball from the 1st box.

    Let us denote \({B_i}\) = The event of transferring a black ball from the \({i^{th}}\) box.

    and, \({W_i}\) = The event of transferring a white ball from the \({i^{th}}\) box.

    \(i=1(1)10\)

    clearly, \(P( {B_1}) = \frac{2018}{4035}\)

    Now, we shall use the Principle of Mathematical Induction to show that this probability remains the same after every ball transfer.

    Suppose, \(P( {B_k}) = \frac{2018}{4035}\)

    then, \(P({ W_k}) = 1 - P( {B_k}) = \frac{2017}{4035}\)

    Now, \(P({ B_{k+1}}) = P({ B_{k + 1}}|{B_k}) P({ B_k}) + P({ B_{k + 1}}|{W_k}) P({ W_k}) = \frac{2019}{4036}\cdot \frac{2018}{4035} + \frac{2018}{4036}\cdot \frac{2017}{4035} = \frac{2018}{4035}\)

    hence, \(P({ B_{10}}) = \frac{2018}{4035}\)



  3. Find all integer values \(a\) such that the quadratic expression \((x+a)(x+1991)+1\) can be factored as a product \((x+b)(x+c)\), where \(b\) and \(c\) are integers.

    4. Answer

    \((x+a)(x+1991)+1=(x+b)(x+c)\)

    Putting \(x= -b\), we get , \((a-b)((b-1991)=1\).

    We note that \((a-b)\) and \((b-1991)\) are both integers. Hence, we have two possible cases.

    Case 1 :\((a-b)=1=(b-1991)\) which gives \(a = 1993\).

    Case 2 : \((a-b)= -1=(b-1991)\) which gives \(a = 1989\).



  4. Each of 10 line segments is longer than 1 cm and shorter than 55 cm. Show that you can select 3 sides of a triangle from among the segments.

    5. Answer

    If possible let the given statement be wrong. Then given any two sides, all other sides must have length greater than the sum of the two given sides. The two smallest sides can be of length 1 cm each.

    Suppose the sides be \({a_1},{a_2},...,{a_{10}}\) where \({a_1}\leqslant {a_2}\leqslant {a_3}\leqslant ...\leqslant {a_{10}}\)

    We shall show that if the given statement is wrong then we must have \({a_{10}}\geqslant 55\) cm.

    To obtain the smallest possible value of \({a_{10}}\) ,we start with \({a_{1}} = 1\) cm and \({a_{2}}=1\) cm.

    \(\Rightarrow {a_3} > 2cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_4} > 3cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_5} > 5cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_6} > 8cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_7} > 13cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_8} > 21cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_9} > 34cm,\)\( \Rightarrow {a_{10}} > 55cm\)

    This contradicts our assumption. Hence, there must exist three sides which form a triangle.
